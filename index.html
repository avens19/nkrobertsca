About

A bit about me.

  • First Time Around

    As a native Calgarian, I was always exposed to the Oil and Gas industry. As such, I attended the University of Alberta, completing a Bachelor of Science in Geology in 2013. The end of my degree coincided with the economic downturn and after struggling in the oil and gas industry for a few years, I decided to pursue my interest in architecture.

  • A Change of Scenery

    In fall of 2016 I began the Architectural Technologies program at SAIT. I greatly enjoyed the classes, and was lucky enough to gain summer employment at Stantec in 2017. This experience was invaluable as I gained industry skills and knowledge, contacts and a clearer view of my career goals.

  • On the Side

    In my spare time I enjoy spending time in the mountains with my dog, traveling with my husband, and experimenting with my cooking skills. You can often find me stuck in a good book, enjoying board games, or playing volleyball and slo-pitch with friends.

Inglewood Ramsay LRT Station

Commercial

The new Green LRT Line is a unique opportunity to create cohesive design and connection through multiple communities in Calgary. SAIT used this premise and created a capstone project for students which is completed in teams of 3. From pre-design through to construction documents, this project emulates the design process of architectural firms. With participation from the City of Calgary Green Line team, this project represents a culmination of the skills studied at SAIT applied to an actual site, client, and product. Team members: Winnie Steetz and David Le.

  • Date: In Progress

Two Story Office Building

Commercial

Modeled in Revit, this project was completed for a building systems class. The focus was on technical details and connections between the different components and technologies throughout the building. It also adheres to the National Building Code 2015 and community bylaws.

  • Date: December 2017

Rideau Road

Residential

This project was completed for a drafting class using AutoCAD. Based on documents drawn by an architecture firm, this assignment was intended to bridge the gap between residential and commercial projects, as is evident in the complex design and details.

  • Date: April 2017

CHBA Competition

Residential

Each year the Calgary Home Builders Association launches a competition at SAIT in which students design a home on a lot in Sunnyside. This submission was designed for a young professional couple and earned an honourable mention in judging. The emphasis of this competition was design rather than technical drafting, and was completed using Sketchup and AutoCAD.

  • Date: April 2017

Library Remodel

Renovation

The samples library at Stantec’s Red Deer office was becoming unusable and overcrowded. My first task as a summer student was to redesign the space with additional storage and functionality; the goal was to create meeting space alongside sample storage. This mini-project was my first foray into interior design.

  • Date: July 2017

Grocery Store

Fit-up

During my summer at Stantec, a grocery store on the O’Chiese First Nation required a fit-up to provide additional services to the community. A convenience store was added to service the future gas bar, which included a pharmacy. Also, a small café was added to the grocery store with a patron seating area.

  • Date: August 2017
